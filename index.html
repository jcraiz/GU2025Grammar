Level 1 Grammar - Unit 1: Pronouns & "To Be"

What are Pronouns?

Pronouns are short words for people or things. In Spanish, like "yo" or "tú". In English, we use them with the verb "to be".

They help us not repeat names. Example: Instead of "The captain is tall. The captain is strong.", say "He is tall. He is strong."

Pronoun Who? Example (Navy)
IMe (yo)I am a sailor.
YouYou (tú or usted)You are on the ship.
HeHe (male, él)He is the captain.
SheShe (female, ella)She is a doctor on base.
ItThing (eso)It is a big boat.
WeWe (nosotros)We are in the Navy.
TheyThey (ellos or ellas)They are my team.

Practice: Say "I am..." about your job.

The Verb "To Be" in Present Simple

The verb "to be" means "ser" or "estar" in Spanish. It describes who you are or where you are now.

Present Simple = now, every day. Forms: am, is, are.

Rule: Use with pronouns. I + am. You/We/They + are. He/She/It + is.

Base sentence: Subject + verb to be + description.

Example: I am ready for duty.

Why learn this? In the Navy, you say: "I am on watch." or "The ship is safe."

Affirmative Sentences (Positive)

Affirmative = yes, true. Use "am/is/are" + description.

Pronoun Form Navy Example
IamI am a lieutenant.
YouareYou are brave.
HeisHe is on patrol.
SheisShe is the chef.
ItisIt is my uniform.
WeareWe are a team.
TheyareThey are officers.

Practice: Make a sentence: "We are... " about your unit.

Negative Sentences

Negative = no, not true. Add "not" after am/is/are.

Short forms: am not / isn't / aren't.

Pronoun Form Navy Example
Iam notI am not late for duty.
Youare notYou are not afraid.
Heis notHe is not the pilot.
Sheis notShe is not on vacation.
Itis notIt is not broken.
Weare notWe are not lost at sea.
Theyare notThey are not new sailors.

Say it fast: I'm not... You're not... He's not... etc.

Practice: "The radio is not..." (think of a problem on ship).

Yes/No Questions

Questions = ask for yes or no. Put am/is/are before the pronoun.

Answer: Yes, I am. / No, I'm not.

Pronoun Question Form Navy Example Answer Example
IAm I...?Am I on watch tonight?Yes, you are.
YouAre you...?Are you ready, sailor?Yes, I am.
HeIs he...?Is he the commander?No, he isn't.
SheIs she...?Is she in the engine room?Yes, she is.
ItIs it...?Is it a submarine?No, it isn't.
WeAre we...?Are we safe now?Yes, we are.
TheyAre they...?Are they on training?No, they aren't.

Practice: Ask a friend: "Are you in the Navy?"

Practice with Navy Examples

Now, mix pronouns and "to be". Talk about your day!

  • I am a sailor in the Colombian Navy. I am strong.
  • You are my friend. You are not tired.
  • He is the captain. Is he happy?
  • She is on the bridge. She is not afraid of the sea.
  • It is our ship. It is big and fast.
  • We are a crew. We are ready for mission.
  • They are officers. Are they from Cartagena?

Your turn: Write 3 sentences about your team. Say one positive, one negative, one question.
